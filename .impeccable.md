## Design Context

### Users
Two overlapping audiences:
- **SMB owners** searching for a developer, comparing options, varying technical literacy - they're evaluating whether to trust this person with a real project
- **Agency partners** (freelance or subcontract work) - more technical, faster to read the signal

Both visit the site with a job to do: decide quickly if Patrick is worth contacting. The site must make that decision easy and compelling. It is not a portfolio gallery - it is a conversion surface.

### Brand Personality
**Fast, direct, no-bullshit.**

Not a big agency. Not a template shop. One expert who knows exactly what he's doing and doesn't pad sentences. The copy is already like this - the design must match. No decorative softness. No corporate stock-photo warmth. No hero with five abstract shapes. The site should feel like the person who built it - a craftsman who cares about speed, sustainability, and clean outcomes.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light - deliberate, not a default. Daytime browsing, professional context.
- **Tone**: Editorial-utilitarian. Think a well-designed technical magazine or a high-quality tool documentation site - purposeful, confident, no wasted space. Not a startup landing page. Not a luxury studio. Something closer to a printed technical brochure with a strong typographic spine.
- **Color**: The cyan/teal primary is likely brand-defining - use it as a sharp accent, not a flood. The multi-palette system (casper, summer-green, amber, rose) should feel like deliberate variation, not random noise.
- **Eco angle**: The CO2 badge and sustainability messaging are genuine differentiators. The design should feel lean and intentional - nothing bloated, nothing decorative for its own sake. Performance IS the brand.
- **Anti-patterns to avoid**: Generic agency grid (icon + heading + blurb × 6), glowing hero gradients, centered everything, rounded cards with drop shadows, any "AI slop" aesthetic. Also avoid the French-startup vibe (everything purple and rounded).

### Design Principles
1. **Personality over polish** - the site must feel like a person, not a template. Patrick's directness should be visible in the layout, not just the copy.
2. **Nothing earns its place without a job** - every element either converts, informs, or establishes credibility. Pure decoration is cut.
3. **Speed as aesthetic** - lean layouts, minimal DOM, zero visual bloat. The site performs the brand promise.
4. **Direct hierarchy** - within 5 seconds a visitor should know: who, what, why call him. No preamble.
5. **Regional confidence** - not trying to look like a global agency. Owns the Annecy/Geneva/Lausanne identity as a strength, not a limitation.
